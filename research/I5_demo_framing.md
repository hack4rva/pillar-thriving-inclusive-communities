# Trust-First Demo Framing for Richmond Resource Navigation

## Executive Summary
Winning hackathon demos in the social impact space succeed by proving real-world usability and trust, not just showcasing code. With typical demo times strictly capped at 3 to 5 minutes [1], or even a 2-minute pitch format [2], your narrative must be ruthlessly efficient. The data shows that 90% to 91% of U.S. adults own a smartphone, and 16% are "smartphone-only" internet users who lack home broadband [3] [4] [5]. Therefore, a mobile-first, offline-capable demonstration is non-negotiable. 

Furthermore, judges are increasingly wary of "techno-solutionism"—the flawed belief that technology alone can fix complex social issues [6] [7] [8]. To score highly across the rubric (D1-D8), your framing must position your prototype as a trust-building bridge to existing community resources like Help1RVA, rather than a standalone cure-all. This report provides a structured, second-by-second narrative plan, opening and closing scripts, and a clear guide on how to avoid overclaiming impact.

## Rubric-to-Narrative Mapping

To maximize your score, every rubric dimension must be paired with a visible "proof move" during the live demo. 

| Rubric Dimension | What Judges Need | Your Live Demo Move | Supporting Evidence / Source |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **D1 (Clarity)** | A specific person and problem | 20–30s opening story: "María, 34, Richmond..." | Human-centered narrative hook |
| **D2 (Scope)** | Explicit limits on what you built | "Out of scope" slide (e.g., no eligibility engines) | SVI Principle 5: Do not overclaim [9] |
| **D3 (Data)** | Named sources and limitations | "Powered by Help1RVA" + manual verification log | Help1RVA lists 1,800+ programs [10] |
| **D4 (Champion)** | Real organization + commitment | Quote/email from YMCA or Homeward for a pilot | YMCA and Homeward utilize Help1RVA [10] [11] |
| **D5 (Population)** | Specific, sourced local statistic | Census QuickFacts % on slide with year | U.S. Census QuickFacts (Richmond City) [12] |
| **D6 (Environment)**| Works on phone, no WiFi | Demo on a low-end Android in airplane mode | 16% of adults are smartphone-only [3] [5] |
| **D7 (Success)** | Concrete user outcome | Live timer: find + call in ≤5 minutes | Bridgespan: focus on vital metrics [13] |
| **D8 (Access)** | Mobile, multilingual, no-login | Toggle Spanish live; no account creation shown | Pew Research mobile adoption data [5] |

## Narrative Structure and Time Allocations

Hackathon demos are unforgiving with time. Major League Hacking (MLH) advises that API demos should be 3-5 minutes maximum [1], while events like Cal Hacks restrict pitches to just two minutes followed by Q&A [2]. Choose the column below that matches your event's rules and rehearse to the second.

| Narrative Beat | 3-Minute Demo | 4-Minute Demo | 5-Minute Demo |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **1. The Person** (Specific, human hook) | 0:20 | 0:30 | 0:30 |
| **2. The Trust Barrier** (What stops them) | 0:20 | 0:30 | 0:30 |
| **3. The Insight** (Trust > Information) | 0:20 | 0:30 | 0:30 |
| **4. The Prototype** (Live demonstration) | 1:20 | 1:45 | 2:15 |
| **5. The Evidence** (Data used/verified) | 0:20 | 0:25 | 0:30 |
| **6. The Limitations** (What you didn't build) | 0:10 | 0:10 | 0:15 |
| **7. Path Forward** (Champion & next steps) | 0:10 | 0:10 | 0:15 |
| **8. Close** (The ask) | 0:10 | 0:10 | 0:15 |

*Strategic Takeaway:* Run a visible timer during the prototype phase to prove your success criterion (e.g., time-to-call ≤ 5 minutes). 

## Operating Environment and Accessibility Proof Kit

Do not just tell the judges your app is accessible; show them. Systems that automate social services can easily become rigid and deny access to vulnerable populations [7] [8]. Prove your flexibility live.

| Capability to Prove | Live Proof in Demo | Fallback if Live Fails |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Mobile-first** | Run demo on a physical low-end Android device | Switch to a pre-recorded screencast |
| **No WiFi / Low Data** | Turn Airplane mode ON; show LTE toggle off | Discuss offline-first cache or SMS option |
| **No-login** | Start fresh; demonstrate immediate access | Show guest mode video |
| **Bilingual (Spanish)** | Toggle language to Spanish; read labels aloud | Show bilingual screenshots |
| **Call Initiation** | 1-tap call to a verified program | Show navigator intake form or 2-1-1 fallback |

## Data Readiness and Verification Plan

To satisfy D3, you must name your sources and admit their limits. 
* **The Backbone:** State that your tool leverages the Help1RVA directory, which contains over 1,800 local programs [10], powered by the findhelp network that boasts at least 1,278 resources in every county [14].
* **The Verification:** Do not just say "we used data." Show a slide detailing your manual verification: "We spot-checked 20 phone numbers and confirmed 5 intake forms."
* **The Population Stat:** Pull the exact "Language other than English spoken at home, percent" or "Foreign-born, percent" from the 2020-2024 U.S. Census Bureau QuickFacts for Richmond city, Virginia [12].

## Champion and Path Forward

Judges reward teams that have a realistic path to deployment. Name a specific champion. For example, the YMCA of Greater Richmond actively utilizes Help1RVA and has a Social Needs Navigation team that contacts users within 48 hours [10]. Alternatively, Homeward directs residents to Help1RVA and Virginia 2-1-1 [11]. Enter the demo with an email or quote from one of these organizations agreeing to a lightweight, 30-day pilot.

## Scripting the Narrative

### Opening Sentence Options by MVP Shape
Start with a human, not a feature.
* **SMS Concierge:** "María, 34, just moved to Richmond. Her phone plan is prepaid, her English is basic, and today she has 10 minutes on a bus to find help. She texts our number."
* **Mobile Web App:** "José finished a night shift near Gilpin Court; his data is out, but his phone still works. He opens our app in Spanish and taps one button: 'Alimentos hoy.'"
* **Voice-first Line:** "Ana shares a phone with her neighbor. She dials a local number and hears, in Spanish, 'Press 1 for food,' and we connect her to a real person."

### Closing Sentence Options
End with a concrete pilot and a learning agenda.
* "In the next 30 days, with YMCA’s Social Needs Navigation team, we’ll pilot with 25 residents, measure time-to-connection under five minutes, and deliver a stale-listing cleanup report to Help1RVA."
* "Our ask: one site, one champion, and one month to prove that a trust-first handoff can turn a 10-step search into a two-tap call—without logins or WiFi."

### What Not to Say (and Better Alternatives)
Avoid overclaiming, which violates Social Value International's Principle 5 [9] and triggers warnings of techno-solutionism [7].

| Don't Say (Overclaiming) | Do Say (Credible & Specific) |
| :--- | :--- |
| "We solved access to social services." | "We reduced the search steps from 10 to 2 for one resident in a live test." |
| "We partnered with the YMCA." *(if untrue)* | "YMCA is our intended pilot champion; we have an email expressing interest." |
| "We used Help1RVA data." | "We queried Help1RVA and manually verified 20 phone numbers." |
| "Our AI builds trust." | "We use simple rules to prioritize live contact and warm handoffs." |
| "All listings are up to date." | "Aggregated data can be stale; we show a fallback to 2-1-1 and log issues for cleanup." |

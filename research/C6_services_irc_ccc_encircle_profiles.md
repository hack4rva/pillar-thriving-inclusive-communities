## 1. International Rescue Committee (IRC) – Richmond, VA

**Status: FULLY ACTIVE**

| Field | Info |
|---|---|
| Website | https://www.rescue.org/united-states/richmond-va |
| Address | 2004 Bremo Rd, Suite 200, Richmond, VA 23226 |
| Phone | (804) 308-9144 |
| Hours | Monday–Friday, 9 AM–5 PM |
| Email | Via website contact form |

### Services
- **Resettlement:** Airport pickup, furnished housing, benefits enrollment, school enrollment, medical referrals, early case management
- **Employment:** Job readiness training, employer matching, financial coaching — goal of self-sufficiency within 6 months
- **Education:** Daily English classes, financial literacy, cultural orientation, life skills workshops, school enrollment advocacy
- **Legal services:** Citizenship, green cards, family reunification, EADs, travel documents
- **Healthcare:** Medical needs assessment, provider connections, health insurance navigation
- **Specialized case management:** Survivors of trafficking, torture, domestic violence, unaccompanied minors

### Eligibility
Refugees (USRAP), asylees, Special Immigrant Visa (SIV) holders (Afghanistan, Iraq), parolees, trafficking survivors, unaccompanied minors. Legal services available to broader low-income immigrant populations.

### Intake
Federal resettlement system assigns clients to IRC Richmond. For legal services and community support, contact the office directly by phone or email.

## 2. Commonwealth Catholic Charities (CCC) – Richmond

> **⚠️ PROGRAM CEASED — Verify before referring anyone to CCC for resettlement**

**Status: RESETTLEMENT PROGRAM FULLY CEASED since early 2025**

| Field | Info |
|---|---|
| Website | https://www.cccofva.org/refugees |
| Main office | 1601 Rolling Hills Dr, Richmond, VA 23229 |
| Main phone | (804) 285-5900 |
| Housing Resource Center | 809 Oliver Hill Way, Richmond, VA 23219 |
| Housing phone | (804) 648-4177 |
| Email | agency@cccofva.org |

### What Happened
Federal executive orders in January–February 2025 suspended, then terminated, CCC's refugee resettlement contracts. CCC laid off 26 staff statewide and halted all new refugee intakes. Between October 2024 and January 2025, the U.S. government had referred 378 individuals to CCC; approximately 60 families arrived just before the halt and are being supported via emergency community fundraising only (CCC needed ~$1,275/family to cover April–May 2025 rent).

### What Is Still Operating
- **Unaccompanied Refugee Minors (URM) foster care program** — active since 1982; serves youth in 7 federal eligibility categories (refugees, asylees, trafficking survivors, SIJs, etc.); foster parents must be 25+, financially stable, and per the church's definition married or single non-cohabitating
- **Dibert Food Pantry and Baby Diaper Closet** at main office (1601 Rolling Hills Dr)
- **Housing Resource Center** (809 Oliver Hill Way)
- Immigration legal services — may still be partially active; confirm directly

### Hackathon Implication
Any service directory tool must flag CCC with a **"Verify current availability — resettlement program suspended"** warning. Do not list CCC as an active resettlement agency.

## 3. enCircle (formerly Lutheran Family Services of Virginia)

**Status: ACTIVE — narrow scope (unaccompanied immigrant children and youth only)**

| Field | Info |
|---|---|
| Website | https://encircleall.org/immigrant-and-refugee-services |
| Address | 4901 Dickens Rd, Suite 115, Richmond, VA 23230 |
| Phone | 804-288-0122 |
| Email | enGage@enCircleAll.org (counseling program) |
| Hours | By appointment |

### Services
- **enGage counseling:** Free counseling (in-person or virtual) for uninsured immigrant children in the Richmond area; bilingual therapist available
- **Transitional foster care:** Short-term foster placement for unaccompanied immigrant children awaiting family reunification
- **Long-term foster care:** For unaccompanied immigrant teens without successful family reunification
- **Coaching & Visitation:** Trauma-informed Visit Coaching and Parent Coaching for families referred via CSA or DSS

### What enCircle Does NOT Provide
Adult refugee resettlement, ESL for adults, employment services, general immigration legal services, housing navigation for adults. **Not a walk-in resource for adult immigrants.**

### Eligibility
Unaccompanied immigrant children in federal custody or awaiting family reunification. Free counseling for uninsured immigrant children in Richmond. Foster parents: enCircle screening required.

## Key Takeaways for Hackathon Teams

1. **IRC Richmond is the anchor.** For any tool directing residents to resettlement or immigration legal services, IRC is the only reliably active comprehensive provider.
2. **CCC requires a status warning.** The URM foster care and food pantry are still running, but the resettlement program is gone. Any hardcoded listing without a caveat could mislead vulnerable people.
3. **enCircle is not a general resource.** Do not include it in adult-facing service finders.
4. **The CCC gap is a real story.** A tool that surfaces this funding gap and connects the 60 legacy families to emergency support options could be genuinely valuable.

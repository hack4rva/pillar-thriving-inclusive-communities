## Local Radio Stations (Parallel.ai Research, 2026-03-26)

### Spanish-Language Stations

**Ultra Radio Richmond — WULT / WVNZ**
- Frequencies: 94.1 FM, 92.9 FM, 1540 AM, 1450 AM (simulcast network)
- Language: Spanish
- Format: Spanish CHR/variety — reggaeton, salsa, bachata, merengue, Latin pop
- Owner: Mobile Radio Partners, Inc. (Mike Mazursky)
- Website: https://ultraradiorichmond.com
- Outreach relevance: **High** — largest Spanish-language reach in Richmond metro; actively used by community orgs (see programs below)

**Radio Poder — WBTK 1380 AM**
- Frequency: 1380 AM
- Language: Spanish
- Format: Spanish Christian/community programming
- Owner: Mount Rich Media
- Website: https://wbtk.com / https://radiopoder.media
- Outreach relevance: **High** — family/faith-oriented; trusted community positioning

### Community Radio

**WRIR-LP 97.3 FM — Richmond Community Radio**
- Language: English (primary); world music and cultural programming
- Format: Volunteer-run; underrepresented music and public affairs
- Website: https://wrir.org
- Outreach relevance: **Moderate** — no immigrant-language programs confirmed; open community access model means orgs can potentially propose new shows

## Specific Community Radio Programs

These named programs were identified by Parallel.ai research — they are key outreach channels for hackathon teams to know about:

|---|---|---|---|---|
| **El Corazón de la Comunidad** | Ultra Radio Richmond (94.1 FM, 1540 AM, 92.9 FM, 1450 AM) | Sacred Heart Center | Weekly | Community information for Spanish-speaking Richmond residents |
| **Todo Latino RVA Weekly Show** | Ultra Radio Richmond (94.1 FM) | Todo Latino RVA | Thursdays, 10:00 AM | Spanish-language community information for local Latine population |

## OIRE Radio Outreach

The City's OIRE has an expanded mission for inclusivity beyond Spanish speakers. However, research found **no formal, standing OIRE radio partnerships**. OIRE's outreach is conducted through partner organizations — it is partner orgs like Sacred Heart Center that independently run radio segments, not OIRE directly.

---

## Non-Spanish Communities: Radio Gap

No licensed, over-the-air radio stations or recurring programs in **Arabic, Burmese, Karen, Nepali, Amharic, or Tigrinya** were identified in the Richmond area.

One notable community finding: An **"RVA Sgaw Karen Radio Program"** was referenced in a community Facebook post (August 2025). This appears to be a community-run online stream, not a licensed broadcast. It signals that the Karen community is creating its own media in the absence of local radio.

**Design implication:** For non-Spanish communities, radio is not a viable outreach channel. WhatsApp broadcast lists and Facebook groups (managed by trusted community messengers) are the recommended alternatives.

## Implications for Hackathon Tools

- Spanish-speaking communities have two dedicated broadcast outlets (Ultra Radio, Radio Poder) plus existing community segments — **partner with Sacred Heart Center to distribute tool information via El Corazón de la Comunidad**
- All non-Spanish communities need WhatsApp and in-person messenger strategies; radio doesn't reach them
- Facebook groups are the highest-reach digital channel across Latino communities; target specific community groups, not just pages
- WhatsApp broadcast lists managed by trusted community messengers (not bots) are the most privacy-safe digital channel
- The "RVA Sgaw Karen Radio Program" on Facebook signals an unmet community media need — a tool that supports community-run audio/video content distribution could be valuable

## Communication Channels

OIRE confirmed the following communication channels used by Richmond immigrant and refugee communities (2026-03-26):

| Channel | Notes |
|---|---|
| Facebook (groups) | Primary digital channel; reaches broad adult populations |
| Instagram | More common among younger community members |
| YouTube | Video content distribution |
| WhatsApp | Direct messaging and community group coordination |
| In-person networks | Word of mouth, community associations, cultural events |
| Local radio stations | Reaches residents with limited digital access |

**Design implication:** Tools targeting immigrant residents should prioritize Facebook groups and WhatsApp for distribution. Staff-facing tools can use standard web/mobile channels. In-person trust relationships remain central — digital tools should complement, not replace, community navigator relationships.

## Usage Guidance for Hackathon Teams

1. **Do not scrape or store individual resident PII** — this list is for building navigation and connection tools, not for collecting data about residents.
2. **These organizations are potential partners and validators**, not just data points — consider reaching out during the hackathon for quick feedback.
3. **Language capacity varies** — not every organization listed serves all languages. Verify language coverage before surfacing an org as a match in multilingual tools.
4. **Trust is earned, not assumed** — tools built for this community should be reviewed with at least one of these organizations before claiming community readiness.

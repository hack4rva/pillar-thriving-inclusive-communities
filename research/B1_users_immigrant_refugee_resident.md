## Key Definitions

Understanding the precise legal classifications is critical for determining employment eligibility and processing requirements.

| Term | Description |
|------|-------------|
| **B-1 COA** | Non-immigrant classification for temporary business visitors. Holders are generally **not** authorized for open-market employment, though limited **C-17** Employment Authorization Documents (EADs) may apply to domestic servants or certain airline employees [1]. |
| **Refugee** | A person who, abroad, meets the definition in INA §101(a)(42) and is admitted under **8 CFR Part 207**. Must be physically present in the U.S. and have **≥ 1 year** residence before adjusting to lawful permanent resident (LPR) status [2] [3]. |
| **Immigrant (Lawful Permanent Resident)** | Person who has been granted a **green card** and may enter or reside in the U.S. as a permanent resident [4]. |
| **Eligible "Other"** | Parolees, Iraqi/Afghan Special Immigrant Visa (SIV) holders, and follow-to-join asylees who receive the same benefits as refugees under the Electronic Disease Notification (EDN) system [4]. |
| **Derivative** | Spouse or child of a principal refugee or immigrant who accompanies or follows-to-join the principal [2]. |

## Eligibility & Admission Process

### B-1 Non-immigrant Eligibility
Individuals with a B-1 Class of Admission (COA) are non-immigrants entering the U.S. temporarily for business. 
* **Entry Requirements:** Must present a valid **I-94** Arrival-Departure Record and an unexpired foreign passport [1].
* **Employment Restrictions:** Employment is strictly limited to **C-17** EAD categories (e.g., domestic servant of a non-immigrant, domestic servant of a U.S. citizen on temporary stay, or certain airline employees) [1]. 
* *Actionable Insight:* Employers and agencies must verify the specific EAD category before authorizing any work or issuing benefits to B-1 visitors.

### Refugee Admission (8 CFR Part 207)
The U.S. refugee admissions program requires rigorous vetting and sponsorship.
* **Eligibility (§ 207.1)**: Applicants must not be **firmly resettled** in a foreign country and must be sponsored by a responsible person or organization [2]. 
* **Processing (§ 207.2-§ 207.4)**: Requires an in-person interview, medical examination, and a sponsorship guarantee. Approval by USCIS outside the U.S. authorizes CBP to admit the applicant conditionally within **4 months** of approval [2]. 
* **Derivatives (§ 207.7)**: Spouses and unmarried minor children may accompany the principal refugee (within 4 months) or follow-to-join later. There is no fee for this benefit request [2]. 

### Adjustment to Lawful Permanent Residence
U.S. immigration law requires refugees to apply for lawful permanent resident status after they have been physically present in the United States for at least 1 year [3].
* **EAD Guidelines:** Refugees do not need to pay fees for an initial or renewal EAD. Furthermore, a refugee can legally work even if their EAD is facially expired, as refugees are authorized to work incident to their status [3]. 
* *Actionable Insight:* When filing Form I-485 (Adjustment of Status) and Form I-765 (EAD) simultaneously, refugees must not file them in the same envelope [3].

## Health Surveillance & Post-Arrival Services

Public health monitoring is a critical component of the immigrant and refugee admission process.

### Electronic Disease Notification (EDN) System
The CDC's EDN system collects health data for **immigrants, refugees, and eligible others** arriving in the United States. A comprehensive surveillance summary covering arrivals from **2014–2019** highlights the system's role in tracking communicable diseases [4].
* **Primary Health Concerns Tracked:** Tuberculosis (TB), syphilis, gonorrhea, and Hansen’s disease [4]. 
* **Examination Types:** Post-arrival medical examinations for refugees and new-arrival immigrants are voluntary and distinct from the mandatory civil-surgeon exams required for adjustment-of-status applicants already in the U.S. [4]. 

### System Modernization and Processing Speeds
Historically, the collection of overseas medical data involved transferring paper records, which took up to 4 weeks and caused delays in notifying state health departments [4].
* **eMedical Integration:** Beginning in 2018, the CDC and the Department of State launched the U.S. version of eMedical. Panel physicians now enter data directly into the system, transferring it to the EDN within **2 days** of the immigrant’s arrival [4].
* *Actionable Insight:* State and local health departments should ensure their systems are optimized to receive and act upon these rapid 2-day electronic notifications to prevent loss to follow-up.

## Documentation Requirements for B-1 Users

Agencies utilizing the Systematic Alien Verification for Entitlements (SAVE) program frequently encounter specific documentation for B-1 visitors and transitioning refugees.

| Document | When Required | Remarks |
|----------|---------------|---------|
| **I-94 Arrival-Departure Record** | At entry | Mandatory for all B-1 holders to prove lawful temporary admission [1]. |
| **Valid Foreign Passport** | At entry | Must be unexpired [1]. |
| **C-17 Employment Authorization Document** | If seeking limited work | Issued only for specific domestic-servant or airline-employee scenarios; does **not** grant open-market employment [1]. |
| **Form I-765 (EAD) – Category (a)(3)** | Refugee-based work authorization | Filed separately from Form I-485; **do not** combine in the same envelope [3]. |
| **Form I-485 (Adjustment of Status)** | After 1 year residence (refugees) | Must include evidence of physical presence and admissibility [3]. |

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

**Can a B-1 visitor work in the U.S.?** 
Generally, no. B-1 visitors are not employment authorized. Only holders of a **C-17** EAD (such as specific domestic servants or airline employees) may perform limited, authorized tasks [1].

**Do refugees need an EAD to work?** 
No. Refugees are authorized to work incident to their status. They may work in the United States even if their EAD is facially expired, though they may choose to renew it under the "(a)(3)" category [3].

**How long does it take for overseas health data to reach U.S. state health departments?** 
Since the implementation of the eMedical system in 2018, data is transferred to the EDN system within **2 days** of the immigrant's arrival in the United States, a significant improvement over the previous 4-week paper process [4].

**What is the priority date for refugee derivatives?** 
The date a refugee application is accepted for filing by USCIS serves as the priority date for case control purposes [2].

**When must a refugee file Form I-485?** 
U.S. immigration law requires refugees to apply for lawful permanent resident status after they have been physically present in the United States for at least **1 year** [3].

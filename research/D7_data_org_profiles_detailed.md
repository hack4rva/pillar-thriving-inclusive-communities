## ReEstablish Richmond

| Field | Info |
|---|---|
| Website | https://www.reestablishrichmond.org |
| Email | info@reestablishrichmond.org |
| Access | By appointment only — no drop-ins; email is fastest |
| Hours | Remote office; appointment-based |

**Services:** Newcomer Navigation (personalized support with English, transportation independence, credential evaluation, employment navigation, personal finance, citizenship prep), Newcomer Education (in-person group classes with childcare/language support), volunteer engagement, newcomer networking, community ESL class directory for Greater Richmond.

**Eligibility:** Newcomer immigrants and refugees in the Richmond area. Not for those in active resettlement case management within their first 3 months (to avoid duplication). Refer a client via the website form.

**Key role:** Acts as a resource hub and connector — not a direct service provider for everything, but knows who does what. The ESL class directory is particularly useful.

## YWCA of Richmond

| Field | Info |
|---|---|
| Website | https://ywcarichmond.org/get-help/ |
| Crisis hotline | **804-612-6126** (24/7, call or text) |
| TTY | Available |
| Access | All services free and confidential, regardless of immigration status |

**Services:** 24/7 crisis hotline and text line, safety planning, emergency shelter, hospital accompaniment, counseling, case management, legal advocacy, workforce navigation, human trafficking survivor response, referrals.

**Language support:** Language line available on the hotline; translators accessible.

**Eligibility:** All people, regardless of immigration status or English ability.

**Note:** YWCA Richmond is primarily a domestic violence and crisis services org. The "Welcome Center" framing in Karla's list likely refers to their welcoming posture toward immigrants, not a named program. Their 24/7 bilingual-accessible hotline is the key resource.

## Waymakers Foundation

| Field | Info |
|---|---|
| Website | https://www.waymakers-foundation.org |
| Distribution hours | Monday 10am–4pm, Tuesday 4–7pm, Friday 10am–2pm |
| Access | Walk-in during distribution hours; contact via text or email for emergency relief |

**Services:** Walk-in choice-based food pantry (culturally relevant groceries for Latino immigrant community), Emergency Relief Services (clothing, burial assistance, rental assistance, diaper assistance, utility assistance) for families who don't qualify for aid elsewhere.

**Community:** Immigrant Latino and undocumented community, Southside Richmond.

**Key role:** Fills gaps for people who fall through other assistance programs. Culturally relevant food (not generic food bank).

## Sacred Heart Center

| Field | Info |
|---|---|
| Website | https://shcrichmond.org |
| Address | 1400 Perry Street, Richmond, VA 23224 |
| Phone | 804-230-4399 |
| Email | hola@shcrichmond.org |

**Services:** ESL classes (Basic–Level 4, morning and evening), GED prep in Spanish, Spanish literacy tutoring, bilingual FAMIS/Medicaid enrollment help, free bilingual medical clinic (2x/month via Bon Secours), domestic violence services (Safe Harbor), food bank, community garden, youth programs, Latino Leadership Institute.

**Key role:** One of Richmond's longest-standing and most comprehensive Latino community hubs. Registration cycles for specific programs.

## La Casa de la Salud (LCS)

| Field | Info |
|---|---|
| Website | https://lacasadelasalud.org |
| Address | 7122 Hull Street Rd, Richmond, VA 23235 |
| Contact | Phone and WhatsApp on website |
| Language | Spanish-forward |

**Services:** Free blood pressure screenings + primary care referrals by Community Health Workers (CHWs), breast/ovarian cancer screening program for high-risk Latina women, referrals for free mammograms and Pap tests (low-income/uninsured), vaccination advocacy (COVID/flu), health education via radio.

**Important:** LCS is a **health bridge org, not a clinic**. It connects Latino residents to health resources — it does not provide direct clinical care.

**Eligibility:** Primarily Latina/Latino community; specific programs have eligibility criteria (income, insurance status).

## Latinos en Virginia Empowerment Center (LIVE / LVEC)

| Field | Info |
|---|---|
| Website | https://latinosenvirginia.org/en |
| Crisis line | **888-969-1825** (24/7, English and Spanish) |
| Office hours | Mon–Fri 9am–4:30pm |
| Address | 9513 Hull Street Rd, Suite B, North Chesterfield, VA 23236 |

**Services:** Domestic and sexual violence crisis support (24/7 bilingual hotline), licensed counseling, survivor support groups, free Spanish/English Interpreter Bank (available to victims AND service providers), telehealth counseling, legal advocacy.

**Key distinction:** Only DV/SV provider in Virginia guaranteeing 24/7 bilingual (Spanish/English) crisis support for Latino community.

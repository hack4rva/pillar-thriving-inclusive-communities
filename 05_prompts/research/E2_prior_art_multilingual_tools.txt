Prior Art E2 — Multilingual Civic Technology Tools

Research multilingual civic technology tools that have successfully served populations with limited English proficiency. Focus on approaches that work at civic or community scale.

Questions to answer:
1. What multilingual civic tools have been built in the US that achieved meaningful adoption among immigrant communities? (Beyond NYC — include mid-size cities)
2. What translation approaches have been used? (Professional translation, community-reviewed translation, machine translation with review, machine translation only) What were the outcomes of each?
3. What languages have been prioritized, and how were priority languages selected?
4. What interface design approaches reduce language burden? (Images, icons, audio, simplified vocabulary, visual navigation)
5. What is the minimum viable multilingual approach for a hackathon prototype? (Spanish only? Category labels only? Full translation?)
6. What are the documented risks of deploying machine-translated civic content? Are there examples of harm from mistranslation?
7. How have successful tools handled the ongoing maintenance of multilingual content as services change?

Output:
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Recommended multilingual approach for a Richmond hackathon team with 48 hours
- Minimum viable translation checklist
- Risk flags for low-quality translation approaches
- What "good enough" looks like for a demo vs. what is required for production deployment

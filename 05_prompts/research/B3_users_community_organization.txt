Users B3 — Trusted Community Organization

Profile the role of trusted community organizations in serving as intermediaries between immigrant and refugee residents and formal service providers.

Research questions:
1. What types of organizations function as trusted intermediaries in immigrant communities? (Faith organizations, cultural associations, ethnic business organizations, community health workers, peer navigators) What makes each type trusted?

2. How do trusted community organizations currently share information about services with their members? What channels do they use? What content formats work?

3. What capacity do these organizations have? (Staff, volunteers, budget, technical capacity) What tools can they realistically use and maintain?

4. What do trusted community organizations need from a tool that supports their information-sharing role? What would make them willing to participate in a messenger distribution system?

5. What are the risks of formalizing or digitizing the trusted intermediary role? How do you support human relationships without turning community leaders into unpaid service workers?

6. In the Richmond context specifically: which organizations are publicly known to serve as trusted intermediaries for immigrant and refugee communities? (Faith communities, cultural organizations, etc. — use publicly available information only)

Output:
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Persona brief for a "trusted community ambassador" who might use a messenger distribution system
- Design requirements for a tool that supports this role without overwhelming the person
- Risks to avoid

Problem Landscape A2 — How Residents Get Lost Between Agencies

Investigate the cross-agency navigation problem for Richmond residents seeking housing stability, workforce support, or reentry services. Focus specifically on the experience of immigrant and refugee residents who must interact with multiple organizations.

Research:
1. What agencies and organizations are typically involved in the support ecosystem for a recently arrived refugee or immigrant family in Richmond? (City DSS, Housing, workforce programs, nonprofit partners, reentry services)
2. What are the documented hand-off points where residents typically get stuck or have to repeat their story?
3. What intake requirements does each major agency type impose? (ID requirements, appointment systems, eligibility documentation)
4. What is the current state of data sharing between Richmond City agencies and nonprofit partners? What legal barriers exist?
5. How does the "repeating your story" problem manifest for immigrant and refugee residents specifically? (Language barriers, distrust, retraumatization)

National context:
- What have other mid-size cities done to reduce cross-agency navigation friction for immigrant populations?
- What approaches have failed and why?

Output:
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns (what cannot be verified publicly)
- A simple text-based map of the typical service pathway (e.g., Organization A → Organization B → Organization C)
- Key friction points and their causes
- What a hackathon team can realistically address vs. what requires multi-year infrastructure work

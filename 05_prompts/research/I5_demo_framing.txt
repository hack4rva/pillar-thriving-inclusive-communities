Demo Strategy I5 — Framing and Narrative Structure

Provide guidance on the overall narrative framing for a Thriving and Inclusive Communities demo. This prompt focuses on how to tell the story in a way that is honest, compelling, and scored well by rubric dimensions.

Rubric alignment for demo framing:
- D1 (Clarity): Open with a specific, named problem and a specific user. Not "immigrant communities" — a specific person in a specific situation.
- D2 (Scope): Be explicit about what is in scope and what is not. Judges reward teams that know what they didn't build.
- D3 (Data Readiness): Name your data sources and their limitations. A team that says "we manually verified 25 phone numbers" is more credible than one that says "we used Help1RVA data."
- D4 (Champion): Name your potential champion and what commitment (however informal) exists.
- D5 (Population/Impact): Use a specific, sourced statistic. Not "lots of immigrants in Richmond" — a census number with a year.
- D6 (Operating Environment): Show that the tool works in the real world — on a phone, without WiFi, in Spanish.
- D7 (Success Criteria): State what success would look like for a real user. Not "improve access" — "a resident finds the right organization and makes a call within 5 minutes."
- D8 (Accessibility): Demonstrate accessibility — mobile, multilingual, no-login.

Narrative structure:
1. The person (not the problem) — specific, human, believable
2. The trust barrier — what stops them from getting help
3. The insight — why this problem is harder than it looks (trust is the barrier, not information)
4. The prototype — what you built and what it does
5. The evidence — what data you used and what you verified
6. The limitations — what you didn't build and why
7. The path forward — who could take this next and what they'd need

Output:
- Narrative structure outline with time allocations
- Opening sentence options for different MVP shapes
- Closing sentence options that land on post-hackathon potential
- What not to say (phrases that undermine credibility or overclaim)

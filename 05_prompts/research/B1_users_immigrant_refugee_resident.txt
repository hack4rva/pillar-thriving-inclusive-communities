Users B1 — Primary Persona: Recent Immigrant or Refugee Seeking Services

Build a detailed, evidence-grounded persona for the primary user of a service discovery tool in the Thriving and Inclusive Communities pillar.

Profile the following:
1. Demographics and arrival context: What is the range of situations? (Formally resettled refugee vs. asylum seeker vs. undocumented economic migrant vs. family reunification) How do these situations affect their relationship to service providers?

2. Technology access and literacy: What devices do people in this situation typically have? What apps are installed? How comfortable are they with web browsing vs. WhatsApp vs. phone calls vs. in-person?

3. Language profile: What level of English proficiency is typical at 3 months, 12 months, 36 months? How does literacy in one's native language affect digital tool use?

4. Trust profile: What are the primary fears when considering contacting a service organization? How have those fears been shaped by experience in country of origin and in the US?

5. Information-seeking behavior: How do people in this situation currently find out about services? Who do they ask first? What causes them to stop looking?

6. Barriers to action: Beyond finding a service, what prevents someone from making contact? (Fear, language, phone anxiety, childcare, transportation, ID requirements)

7. Success definition: What would a successful service connection look like from this person's perspective?

Use verifiable sources where possible (UNHCR, Pew Research, IRC reports, academic studies on immigrant technology use). Label inferences clearly.

Output: A 1-page persona brief with all fields populated or labeled Unknown, followed by source citations.

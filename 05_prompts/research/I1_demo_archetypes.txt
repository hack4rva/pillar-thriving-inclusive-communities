Demo Strategy I1 — Demo Archetypes for Thriving and Inclusive Communities

Describe the strongest demo archetypes for this pillar and what makes each one compelling to judges.

Archetype 1: The Empathy Opener
Start with the user journey. Show a specific resident's situation (from Journey 1 or Journey 2 in user_journeys.md) before showing the tool. Make judges feel the friction before showing the solution.

Why it works: Judges score on D5 (population and impact). Grounding the demo in a specific, real-feeling person's experience makes the impact tangible.

Archetype 2: The Side-by-Side Contrast
Show the current experience (Help1RVA, Google search in Spanish, a dead phone number) alongside the prototype. Make the contrast visual and immediate.

Why it works: Communicates the problem and solution simultaneously without requiring the judge to imagine the problem on their own.

Archetype 3: The Trust Demonstration
Open with the privacy statement. Explain why it matters. Show that the entire user flow requires zero personal information. Make trust the product.

Why it works: For this pillar specifically, trust is the defining constraint. A team that demonstrates they understood this will score higher on D8 (accessibility) and stand out from teams that built generic service finders.

Archetype 4: The Pathway Walk
For cross-agency navigation tools, walk through the pathway map step by step. Name the friction points. Show what data is missing and what the tool does with what exists.

Why it works: Demonstrates sophistication about the D3=1 constraint. Judges respect teams that name hard problems honestly.

For each archetype:
- What does the demo sequence look like?
- What should be on screen at each point?
- What is the single most compelling moment?
- What is the likely judge question and how to answer it?

Output:
- Four archetype descriptions with demo sequence outlines
- Recommendation: which archetype for which MVP shape
- How to combine archetypes for a 5-minute demo

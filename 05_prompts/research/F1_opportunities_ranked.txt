Opportunities F1 — Ranked Solution Opportunities

Based on the research across sections A through E, rank the solution opportunities for this pillar from most to least promising for a Richmond hackathon team.

Evaluate each opportunity against:
1. Problem clarity (is the user and pain point well-defined?)
2. Data availability (can the team get usable data in 2–3 hours?)
3. Weekend feasibility (can a working demo exist by Sunday?)
4. Privacy safety (does the approach avoid collecting vulnerable data?)
5. Post-hackathon potential (is there a realistic continuation path?)
6. Alignment with rubric (does it directly address the targeted problem statements?)

Opportunities to evaluate:
A. Privacy-first service finder (anonymous browsing, plain language, no login)
B. Multilingual help resource tool (Spanish + other languages, voice-friendly)
C. Trusted community messenger distribution system (WhatsApp/SMS via ambassadors)
D. Case manager coordination helper (staff-facing referral reference tool)
E. Cross-agency service pathway map (visual map of services and handoffs)
F. Help1RVA interface improvement prototype (redesigned front end)
G. Language access audit tool (surfaces language access gaps in current service ecosystem)

Output:
- Ranked list with scores on each dimension
- Recommended primary choice with rationale
- Recommended secondary choice (if team wants a complement)
- What each opportunity explicitly does NOT address (so teams are honest about scope)

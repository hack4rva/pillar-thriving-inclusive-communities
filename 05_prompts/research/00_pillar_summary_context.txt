Pillar Summary Context — Thriving and Inclusive Communities

This file provides framing context for all research prompts in this directory. Read this before running any prompt to understand the constraints and goals.

Pillar: 5 — Thriving and Inclusive Communities
Hackathon: Richmond Civic Hackathon, March 27–29, 2026, VCU Snead Hall
Working session: March 6, 2026

Core problem space:
Richmond has a growing immigrant and refugee population. Many residents struggle to safely discover and access community support services because of language barriers, fear of data exposure, and fragmented information. Services exist — the gap is safe, accessible, navigable delivery.

Two targeted problem statements:
1. Help Residents Safely Discover and Connect to Trusted Support Services (24/32 — Strong)
2. Helping Residents Navigate the Right Support Services Without Repeating Their Story (22/32 — Needs work; D3=1 critical data gap)

Defining constraint for all research:
Privacy and trust are the primary barriers, not lack of services. Any research finding that recommends collecting personal data from immigrant or refugee residents must be flagged as high-risk.

Key organizations in Richmond for this pillar:
- Help1RVA: Community resource directory (help1rva.com)
- ReEstablish Richmond: Refugee resettlement
- YMCA of Greater Richmond: Workforce and community programming
- Neighborhood Community Services (NCS): Community support coordination
- FindHelp / Aunt Bertha: National service directory platform
- UniteUs: Closed-loop referral platform

Priority languages (unverified — research should confirm):
Spanish, Arabic, Nepali, Burmese

Output standards for all prompts:
- Facts: labeled Verified with source title and URL
- Inferences: labeled Inference with rationale
- Unknowns: labeled Unknown — do not invent data to fill gaps
- Privacy implications: flag any finding that has data collection or exposure implications

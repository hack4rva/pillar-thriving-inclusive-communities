Services C4 — Fragmentation Between Organizations and Data Siloing

Investigate the fragmentation problem in Richmond's immigrant and refugee service ecosystem. Focus on how data siloing, lack of interoperability, and organizational autonomy create friction for residents and case managers.

Questions to answer:
1. What service categories have the most fragmentation in Richmond — where residents must contact multiple organizations without a clear coordination structure?
2. How do Help1RVA, FindHelp, and UniteUs co-exist in Richmond? Which organizations use which platforms? Is there duplication or gaps?
3. What happens when a referral is made from one organization to another? How is the outcome tracked? How does the referring organization know if the resident connected?
4. What are the specific organizational or contractual barriers that prevent data sharing? (HIPAA, funder requirements, organizational autonomy, lack of shared technology standards)
5. What are the human costs of fragmentation — for residents (repeating story, lost time, missed benefits) and for case managers (coordination overhead, burnout)?
6. What have Richmond or comparable cities done to reduce service fragmentation without requiring full integration?

Output:
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Fragmentation map: which gaps are most harmful and most addressable?
- What a hackathon team can do to surface or reduce fragmentation without requiring cross-agency data access

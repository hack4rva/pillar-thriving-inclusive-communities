Risks G1 — The Risk of False Safety: Tools That Give Immigrants False Confidence

Investigate the specific risk that a service discovery or navigation tool could give immigrant residents false confidence about their safety — leading them to take actions that expose them to harm they would have avoided without the tool.

Risk scenarios to analyze:
1. A tool says "we don't collect your information" but actually logs IP addresses, device fingerprints, or session data that could be linked to an individual under legal process
2. A tool displays "no papers required" for an organization without verifying that the organization's current policy actually guarantees this
3. A tool presents itself as "safe" through visual design choices (green checkmarks, shield icons) that imply a level of protection the tool does not actually provide
4. A tool claims anonymity but is hosted on infrastructure owned by an entity subject to law enforcement data requests (government cloud, third-party analytics)
5. A tool uses an AI chatbot that is inconsistently accurate, giving some residents correct guidance and others incorrect guidance based on query phrasing
6. A tool is shared through WhatsApp and forwarded to communities that misinterpret its scope ("this tool can help you with your immigration case")

For each scenario:
- What is the specific harm mechanism?
- What design or process change prevents it?
- Is this risk avoidable with weekend-scale resources, or does it require legal review and infrastructure decisions?

Output:
- Risk register for false safety scenarios
- Design guardrails for each risk
- Minimum viable safety review before a tool is shared with vulnerable community members
- Language for a responsible disclaimer in the tool itself

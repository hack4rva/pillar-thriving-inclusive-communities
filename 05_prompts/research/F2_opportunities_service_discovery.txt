Opportunities F2 — Service Discovery Opportunity Space

Deep dive on the service discovery opportunity — building a tool that helps immigrant and refugee residents safely find services.

Map the full opportunity space:
1. What is the minimum viable version of a service discovery tool that would be genuinely useful to a resident with limited English proficiency and privacy concerns?
2. What features add the most value per hour of build time? (Prioritize ruthlessly)
3. What is the ideal information architecture? How should services be categorized to match how residents think about their needs vs. how agencies categorize their programs?
4. How should the privacy statement be worded to be both legally accurate and intuitively trustworthy to a resident who reads it?
5. What signals of trustworthiness (beyond words) can a service finder communicate? (Organizational logos, endorsements, visual design)
6. How should the tool handle "no results"? What should it show when a category has limited Richmond coverage?
7. What is the simplest multilingual approach for a hackathon prototype? Spanish-first? Image-based categories? Audio labels?

Compare approaches:
- Static JSON + simple frontend vs. live Help1RVA data sync
- Category browsing vs. free-text search vs. guided question flow
- Showing all organizations vs. filtering to "most trusted" or "no papers required"

Output:
- Recommended information architecture
- Recommended feature set for MVP (must-have vs. nice-to-have)
- Recommended tech approach with rationale
- Privacy statement draft (200 words maximum, plain language)

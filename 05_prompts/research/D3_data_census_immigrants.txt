Data D3 — ACS and Census Data on Richmond Immigrant Populations

Research and extract relevant Census and American Community Survey data for Richmond's immigrant and refugee population.

Data to find:
1. Total foreign-born population in Richmond city and the Richmond MSA (most recent ACS 5-year estimates)
2. Countries of origin — top 10 for Richmond city
3. Language spoken at home — distribution of non-English languages
4. English language proficiency levels (speaks English "less than very well") by language community
5. Year of entry — how many arrived in the past 5 years vs. longer-established residents
6. Geographic distribution within Richmond — which zip codes or neighborhoods have the highest immigrant population concentrations?
7. Socioeconomic indicators — income, poverty rate, employment for foreign-born residents vs. native-born

Sources:
- data.census.gov (ACS 5-year, Table B05001 nativity, B16001 language, B05011 year of entry)
- Virginia Department of Health or planning department analysis if available
- PlanRVA regional planning data

For language specifically:
- What are the top 5 non-English languages spoken in Richmond?
- Which communities have the lowest English proficiency rates?

Output:
- Key statistics with source citations (table number, year, geographic level)
- A simple demographic context table for hackathon teams
- Caveats on data currency and geographic precision
- Links to specific Census data tables for teams to explore further

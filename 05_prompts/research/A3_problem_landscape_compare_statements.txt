Problem Landscape A3 — Comparing the Two Targeted Problem Statements

Compare the two targeted problem statements for the Thriving and Inclusive Communities pillar:

Statement 1: Help Residents Safely Discover and Connect to Trusted Support Services (Score 24/32 — Strong)
Statement 2: Helping Residents Navigate the Right Support Services Without Repeating Their Story (Score 22/32 — Needs work; D3=1 critical data gap)

For each statement, assess:
1. Clarity of the user: Who exactly is the primary user? How well-defined is their problem?
2. Data availability: What data exists that a hackathon team could actually use? What is missing?
3. Software-shapedness: Can software meaningfully address this problem without requiring policy change, data integration, or replacing human relationships?
4. Weekend feasibility: What is the most ambitious but realistic MVP achievable in 48 hours?
5. Post-hackathon path: What would need to happen for a prototype to become a real tool used by real residents?

Then compare the two statements:
- Which one is stronger for a hackathon?
- Which one has more post-hackathon potential if a champion is identified?
- Is there a single prototype concept that credibly addresses both?

Output:
- Side-by-side comparison table
- Recommendation with rationale
- One MVP concept that addresses the stronger statement
- One MVP concept that addresses both statements partially

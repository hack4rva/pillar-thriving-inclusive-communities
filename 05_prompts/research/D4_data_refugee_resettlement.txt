Data D4 — Virginia Refugee Resettlement Data and ORR Data

Research available data on refugee resettlement in Virginia and Richmond specifically.

Data to find:
1. Annual refugee arrivals to Virginia — how many per year for the past 5 years?
2. Where in Virginia do refugees resettle? What share goes to the Richmond area?
3. Which resettlement agencies operate in Richmond and what are their capacities?
4. What countries of origin are most represented among Virginia refugees in recent years?
5. What services are mandated for refugees in the first 90 days of arrival? (Reception and Placement, Matching Grant, other ORR programs)
6. What is the Office of Refugee Resettlement (ORR) data on Virginia? What is publicly available at the federal level?

Sources:
- Virginia Department of Social Services — Office of New Americans (ons.virginia.gov or dss.virginia.gov)
- US Department of Health and Human Services / Office of Refugee Resettlement (acf.hhs.gov/orr)
- Refugee Processing Center (wrapsnet.org) — public data on arrivals by state
- State Refugee Coordinator reports (if public)

For Richmond specifically:
- What resettlement agencies are publicly listed as operating in Richmond?
- Is ReEstablish Richmond the primary agency? Are there others?
- What capacity do Richmond-area agencies have?

Output:
- Available data with source citations
- Data gaps (what would require direct contact with Virginia DSS or a FOIA request)
- Population estimates a hackathon team can cite in their demo
- Caveats on the difference between "refugees" (formal resettlement program) and the broader immigrant population

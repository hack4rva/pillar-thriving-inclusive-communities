Data D1 — Help1RVA Data: What Is Accessible, How to Extract, Format

Investigate what data is available from Help1RVA and how a hackathon team would access it.

Technical investigation:
1. Does Help1RVA have a public API? If so, what endpoints are available and what format do they return?
2. Is there a bulk data export or download function?
3. What data fields are present for each organization listing? (Name, address, phone, categories, hours, languages, eligibility notes, description)
4. How is the data structured? (Flat list, hierarchical categories, geographic indexing)
5. What is the data quality like? (Completeness of phone numbers, currency of listings, accuracy of language flags)
6. Are there any terms of service that would restrict scraping or re-use of Help1RVA data?

Alternative access paths:
1. If no API is available, what is the minimum viable manual scraping process for a hackathon team?
2. What subset of the Help1RVA data would be most valuable for a 20–30 organization curated list focused on immigrant/refugee services?
3. Is there a CSV or JSON export of community resources from the City of Richmond Open Data portal that covers similar content?

For the hackathon:
1. What is the fastest path to a usable dataset of 20–30 relevant Richmond services?
2. What fields must be manually verified (phone numbers, language capacity, intake requirements)?

Output:
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Step-by-step data acquisition plan for a hackathon team (2-3 hours maximum)
- Data quality risks and verification steps

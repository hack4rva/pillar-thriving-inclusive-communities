Risks G2 — PII Collection Risks, HIPAA, and Immigration Enforcement Risks

Provide a detailed analysis of the data collection and exposure risks relevant to tools built for immigrant and refugee communities in Richmond.

PII exposure risks:
1. What constitutes PII in the context of a service navigation tool for immigrants? (Name, address, phone — obvious; IP address, device fingerprint, location — less obvious but equally risky for this population)
2. What legal frameworks govern data collection from this population? (HIPAA for health services, FERPA for education, Virginia Consumer Data Protection Act, general privacy law)
3. Under what circumstances could data collected by a civic tech tool be accessed by law enforcement? (Subpoena, warrant, FOIA, platform data requests)
4. What is the specific risk to undocumented residents if their service-seeking behavior is logged? (ICE enforcement, employment consequences, family separation risk)
5. How does the current federal immigration enforcement climate affect the risk calculus for data collection?

HIPAA specifically:
1. What does HIPAA cover? Does it protect data collected by a civic tech service directory tool?
2. What does HIPAA require of health service providers that participate in a service directory?
3. What health-adjacent data types (behavioral health, substance abuse, reproductive health) have additional legal protections beyond HIPAA?

Privacy-by-design requirements:
1. What is the minimum privacy architecture for a tool serving this population?
2. What hosting and infrastructure choices reduce exposure risk?
3. What analytics tools are acceptable vs. unacceptable for this use case?

Output:
- Risk assessment organized by risk type
- Minimum privacy requirements for a responsible tool in this context
- Specific technology recommendations (hosting, analytics, data handling)
- What must be reviewed by a lawyer before deployment to real users

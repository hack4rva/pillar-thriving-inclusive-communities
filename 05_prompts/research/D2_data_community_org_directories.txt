Data D2 — Community Organization Directories That May Be Accessible

Identify existing directories or lists of community organizations serving immigrant and refugee residents in Richmond that a hackathon team could use as a starting point.

Sources to investigate:
1. United Way of Greater Richmond and Petersburg — partner/agency directory
2. Richmond Community Foundation — grantee list with focus areas
3. Virginia State Refugee Program / Office of New Americans — partner organization list
4. Catholic Charities of Eastern Virginia — public program listings
5. World Relief Richmond (if present) — public service listings
6. Richmond City School District — community resource lists for families
7. VCU Health community benefit reports — listed partner organizations
8. The Valentine Museum or other Richmond cultural institutions — immigrant community documentation
9. Local media coverage (Richmond Times-Dispatch, RVA Mag) — mentions of organizations serving immigrant communities
10. 211 Virginia partner directory — Richmond area

For each source:
- Is a structured list available (CSV, table, directory page)?
- What fields are included?
- How current is the information?
- What are the access conditions?

Output:
- Ranked list of data sources by usability for a hackathon team
- Which sources could be combined to create a starting 20–30 organization list
- Gaps: what types of organizations are likely missing from all of these sources

Risks G4 — Cross-Agency Data Sharing Walls (HIPAA, Policy, Technical)

Analyze the specific barriers to cross-agency data sharing in Virginia that create the D3=1 constraint for Statement 2.

Legal barriers:
1. HIPAA: What does HIPAA protect? How does it apply to data sharing between City social services, health programs, and nonprofit providers?
2. 42 CFR Part 2: What additional protections apply to substance use disorder treatment records? Why are these particularly relevant for reentry services?
3. FERPA: How does it affect data sharing when school-age children are part of the client household?
4. Virginia state law: Does Virginia have state-level privacy laws that affect social service data sharing beyond federal requirements?
5. Confidentiality of public assistance information: What Virginia code provisions govern DSS client data confidentiality?

Policy and organizational barriers:
1. What organizational policies at City DSS, Housing, and workforce programs limit data sharing with nonprofits?
2. What is the typical data sharing agreement process between Richmond City agencies and nonprofit partners? How long does it take?
3. What contractual obligations from program funders (HUD, HHS, workforce development funding) restrict data sharing?

Technical barriers:
1. What case management systems do Richmond-area organizations use? Are they compatible?
2. What would technical interoperability between Help1RVA, FindHelp, and UniteUs require?

For hackathon teams:
1. Which of these barriers can a hackathon prototype work around entirely?
2. Which barriers require advocacy or policy change — and how should a team frame this?

Output:
- Categorized barrier analysis (legal, policy, technical)
- What a hackathon team can build without crossing any of these walls
- How to talk about these barriers credibly in a demo without being either dismissive or defeatist

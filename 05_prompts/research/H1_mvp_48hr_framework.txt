MVP Feasibility H1 — 48-Hour Build Framework for Thriving and Inclusive Communities

Provide a practical framework for scoping an MVP in the Thriving and Inclusive Communities pillar that can be built and demoed in 48 hours.

Framing questions:
1. Given the trust and privacy constraints, what is the maximum scope that can be responsibly built in a weekend? What corners should NOT be cut?
2. What is the minimum viable prototype that demonstrates the core insight of this pillar — that trust is the barrier, not information?
3. How should a team allocate 48 hours across research/data prep, build, and polish/demo prep?

Recommended time allocation for a 3-5 person team:
- Friday evening (3–4 hours): Problem selection, team skill mapping, data source identification, initial data collection
- Saturday morning (4 hours): Data prep and curation, core build begins
- Saturday afternoon (4 hours): Core build continues, privacy statement drafted, first functional prototype
- Saturday evening (3 hours): Integration, multilingual content (if applicable), risk review
- Sunday morning (3 hours): Polish, testing on mobile, demo preparation
- Sunday noon: Demo ready

For each time block, provide:
- Primary task
- Who on the team should own it
- What done looks like
- What to skip if behind

Output:
- Hour-by-hour framework
- Decision rules for scope cuts (what to sacrifice, what is non-negotiable)
- The single most important thing to finish by Saturday night
- Signs that a team is over-scoped and needs to cut

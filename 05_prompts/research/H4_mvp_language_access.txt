MVP Feasibility H4 — Adding Multilingual Support in 48 Hours

Practical guide for adding meaningful multilingual support to a service discovery or navigation prototype within a 48-hour hackathon.

Scope decisions:
1. Languages: Should a hackathon team attempt Spanish only, or Spanish + one other language?
2. Coverage: Full translation of all content, or priority content only (category labels, privacy statement, top 10 organizations)?
3. Quality: Human review of all translated content, review of only high-risk content, or machine translation with disclosure?
4. Interface: Text toggle button, browser language detection, or both?

Translation approach options ranked by risk:
A. Professional translation service (e.g., Gengo, Rev) — highest quality but takes 24–48 hours and costs money; book in advance
B. Bilingual team member reviews machine translation — good quality if reviewer is available
C. Community organization review — ideal but requires advance coordination
D. AI translation (GPT-4, DeepL) with manual spot-check — acceptable for demo if clearly labeled
E. Google Translate with no review — not recommended for this population; misleading health/social service content is a real risk

What content must be human-reviewed:
- Any statement about privacy or data handling
- Any statement about eligibility or required documentation ("no papers required")
- Emergency contact information
- Descriptions of legal aid or immigration services

What can be machine-translated for demo:
- Category labels and navigation
- Organization names and general descriptions
- Button labels and UI text

Output:
- Recommended multilingual approach for a 48-hour build
- Minimum content coverage for a credible multilingual demo
- Translation checklist with content categories and required vs. acceptable review level
- How to label machine-translated content honestly in the UI
- Cost estimate for professional translation of minimum viable content set

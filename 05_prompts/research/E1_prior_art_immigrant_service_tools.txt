Prior Art E1 — National Tools for Immigrant Service Access

Research national tools, platforms, and approaches that have been built to help immigrant and refugee populations access services. Focus on what works, what doesn't, and what is directly replicable for a Richmond hackathon.

Tools to research:
1. NYC Immigrant Services portal (nyc.gov/immigrants)
2. USCRI (US Committee for Refugees and Immigrants) digital tools
3. IRC (International Rescue Committee) digital products
4. Welcoming America community tools
5. World Relief digital resource tools
6. Any Virginia-specific immigrant service navigation tools

For each tool, assess:
- Target population
- Core functionality
- Technology approach (web, SMS, WhatsApp, app)
- Language support
- Privacy approach
- Evidence of adoption or effectiveness
- What made it succeed or fail
- What is directly replicable in a weekend hackathon

Output:
- Comparative table: Tool, Population, Features, Technology, Privacy, Evidence, Replicability
- Top 3 design patterns that work for this population
- Top 3 design anti-patterns that should be avoided
- Recommendation for Richmond hackathon teams: which patterns to replicate

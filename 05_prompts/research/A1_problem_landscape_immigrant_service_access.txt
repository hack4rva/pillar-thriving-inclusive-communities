Problem Landscape A1 — Immigrant and Refugee Service Access Reality

Investigate the real-world shape of the service access problem for immigrant and refugee residents in Richmond, Virginia. Using only verifiable public information and clearly labeled inferences, explain where the difficulty actually occurs: finding relevant services, understanding what is available, navigating intake requirements, or making contact with organizations safely.

Focus on:
- The specific barriers that cause residents to not seek help even when they need it
- How trust and fear of data exposure function as primary access barriers (not just information gaps)
- The difference between "services exist" and "services are accessible" for this population
- What happens when a resident gives up on seeking help — what are the downstream consequences?

Output:
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Unknowns (what cannot be verified publicly)
- Assessment of primary friction points in service discovery
- Which friction points are addressable by a software tool vs. require human or policy intervention

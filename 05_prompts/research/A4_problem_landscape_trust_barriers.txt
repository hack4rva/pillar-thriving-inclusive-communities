Problem Landscape A4 — Why Trust Is the Core Issue, Not Just Information Access

Investigate the trust dimension of service access for immigrant and refugee communities. This prompt focuses on why trust barriers persist even when services are available and information is theoretically accessible.

Research questions:
1. What research exists on how undocumented immigrants or recently arrived refugees assess whether it is safe to contact a service organization? What factors increase or decrease perceived safety?

2. What specific design or interaction patterns cause immigrant residents to abandon service-seeking behavior? (Examples: forms that ask for address, websites that look like government applications, services that share data with other agencies)

3. What is the documented relationship between immigration enforcement climate and service access behavior? How do policy changes at the federal or state level affect whether residents seek help?

4. What have trusted community organizations (faith communities, cultural organizations, immigrant advocacy groups) done that government and nonprofits have not been able to replicate in terms of trust-building?

5. What communication patterns signal trustworthiness to immigrant communities? (Who delivers the message, how it is formatted, what language it uses)

6. Are there documented examples of digital tools that successfully earned trust from immigrant or refugee communities? What design choices made them trusted?

Output:
- Facts (with URLs)
- Inferences (clearly labeled)
- Key design principles that emerge from the research
- What a hackathon tool can do in 48 hours to begin earning trust
- What trust-building requires beyond technology (human relationships, time, community endorsement)

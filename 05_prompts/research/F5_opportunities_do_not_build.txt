Opportunities F5 — What NOT to Build

This prompt identifies solution directions that should be avoided — either because they require data or infrastructure that doesn't exist, create unacceptable risk for vulnerable users, or have been tried elsewhere and failed.

DO NOT BUILD: CATEGORY 1 — PII and Immigration Status Collection
- Tools that require residents to enter name, address, phone, or email to access service information
- Tools that ask about immigration status, household composition, or income to "personalize" results
- Tools that create user accounts or profiles linked to individual residents
- Tools that log search queries in ways that could be tied to an individual

Why: This data could be subpoenaed. Even if the developer intends to protect it, legal processes, data breaches, or organizational changes could expose it. The harm to a vulnerable resident could be life-altering.

DO NOT BUILD: CATEGORY 2 — Eligibility Determination
- Tools that tell residents whether they qualify for a specific program
- Tools that filter services based on eligibility criteria entered by the resident
- Tools that use rules engines to match residents to benefit programs

Why: Eligibility rules are complex, change frequently, and require trained caseworkers to apply correctly. An incorrect eligibility determination from a software tool could cause a resident to miss a critical benefit or be wrongly turned away.

DO NOT BUILD: CATEGORY 3 — Agency System Integration
- Tools that claim to integrate with City DSS, Housing, or workforce program databases
- Tools that attempt to read or write to internal agency case management systems
- Tools that propose to share resident data between agencies without existing data sharing agreements

Why: HIPAA, state law, and organizational policy create walls around this data. Any "integration" that a hackathon team builds in a weekend would be either fake or a serious compliance risk.

DO NOT BUILD: CATEGORY 4 — Automated Decision Making for Vulnerable Residents
- Tools that make referral decisions without human review
- Chatbots that give specific legal, immigration, or benefits advice
- AI systems that route residents to services without disclosing the AI's limitations

Why: The consequences of an incorrect automated decision are serious. Residents in crisis need reliable guidance, not a high-confidence wrong answer.

Output:
- Explanation of each category with rationale
- For each category, a "what to build instead" alternative that addresses the same underlying need without the risk

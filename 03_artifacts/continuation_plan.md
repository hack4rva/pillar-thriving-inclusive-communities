# Continuation Plan - Thriving and Inclusive Communities

Purpose: Help teams think about what happens after Sunday. A hackathon prototype is not a deployed service. This plan identifies realistic continuation paths, required partners, and immediate next steps for any team that wants their work to have lasting impact.

---

## The continuation challenge for this pillar

Both targeted problem statements have a quick-kill flag: no confirmed departmental champion. Without a named individual at a City agency or anchor organization willing to own post-hackathon continuation, a prototype has no natural home.

This does not mean continuation is impossible — it means teams must actively identify a champion during the hackathon weekend, not after.

## Continuation paths by concept

### Concept 1 — Privacy-First Service Finder
**30 days**: Deploy to a live URL. Submit to Help1RVA or community orgs for feedback. Get one organization to review the privacy language.
**60 days**: Incorporate feedback. Get one community organization to reference the tool in their materials.
**90 days**: Identify a partner organization to host and maintain the tool. Establish a content update process.
**Long-term home**: Help1RVA, a local nonprofit, or a volunteer civic tech group like Code for RVA.

### Concept 2 — Multilingual Help Resource Tool
**30 days**: Complete Spanish translation with human review. Deploy to a live URL.
**60 days**: Add Arabic or Dari support for top categories. Test with community members through a trusted organization. (Nepali is not a confirmed priority language; see research/D6_data_communication_channels.md.)
**90 days**: Identify a multilingual community organization to co-maintain content.
**Long-term home**: A multilingual community organization or Help1RVA with a multilingual content strategy.

### Concept 3 — Trusted Community Messenger System
**30 days**: Identify one or two community ambassadors willing to pilot the system. Run a small test distribution.
**60 days**: Evaluate message accuracy, reach, and ambassador feedback. Refine content update process.
**90 days**: Expand to 5–10 ambassadors across different language communities.
**Long-term home**: A coalition of community organizations willing to co-manage the ambassador network and content.

### Concept 4 — Case Manager Coordination Helper
**30 days**: Share the prototype with 2–3 case managers at YMCA, ReEstablish Richmond, or NCS. Collect feedback on data gaps.
**60 days**: Incorporate language capacity and intake requirement data based on direct interviews.
**90 days**: Establish a quarterly update process. Identify which organization will maintain the tool.
**Long-term home**: A consortium of service providers, or United Way of Greater Richmond & Petersburg.

### Concept 5 — Cross-Agency Service Pathway Map
**30 days**: Share with City of Richmond's Office of Community Wealth Building and/or NCS. Collect corrections to the pathway.
**60 days**: Refine with verified data from a working session with participating organizations.
**90 days**: Publish as an advocacy and onboarding tool for new case managers and community navigators.
**Long-term home**: City agency or community foundation as an orientation document.

## What to preserve from the hackathon

Regardless of concept, preserve:
- The service data you compiled (organizations, contact info, language capacity, intake requirements)
- Any plain-language descriptions you wrote for services
- The privacy statement language you tested
- Any feedback from community members or case managers

This data has value beyond any single tool. If the prototype does not continue, the data can be donated to Help1RVA or shared with partner organizations.

---

## Risks to continuation

1. **No champion named**: The biggest risk. Identify a potential champion before Sunday.
2. **Data becomes stale**: Service directories go out of date within months without active maintenance. Build a maintenance process into the handoff plan.
3. **Community trust not established**: A tool launched without community input may not be adopted even if it is technically functional. Plan for community feedback before going live.
4. **Legal/privacy review not completed**: The privacy statement and data handling approach should be reviewed by an attorney or digital rights organization before the tool is used by vulnerable residents at scale.
5. **Hackathon visibility fades**: Keep the prototype visible by posting it to civic tech channels (Code for America, Code for RVA) and sharing with local media.

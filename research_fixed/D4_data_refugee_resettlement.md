## Executive Summary

The latest UNHCR and IOM data show a modest rebound in refugee resettlement after the sharp declines of 2020‑2022. In 2024, **188,800** refugees were resettled worldwide, surpassing the 2023 figure by approximately 13% [1]. The United States admitted **100,060** refugees in FY 2024, exceeding the combined totals of FY 2022 and FY 2023 and meeting 80% of its statutory ceiling [2]. However, UNHCR projects that more than **2.9 million** people will need resettlement in 2025, highlighting a widening gap between demand and programme capacity [3].

**Key observations**

* The "need" figure for 2025 dwarfs actual resettlement capacity, indicating a severe capacity gap that will intensify unless funding and partner engagement increase.

## Projected Global Needs – 2025

UNHCR’s *Projected Global Resettlement Needs 2025* estimates:

* More than **2.9 million** refugees worldwide will need resettlement next year [3]. 
* The **Asia and Pacific** region continues to have the largest resettlement needs globally, with 776,500 refugees, an increase by about 6 per cent compared to 2024 [6]. 

### Gap Analysis (2024‑2025)

| 2024 Resettlement Capacity | 2025 Projected Need | Shortfall | % of Need Met |
|----------------------------|---------------------|-----------|---------------|
| 188,800 (actual) [1] | 2,900,000 [3] | 2,711,200 | **6.5%** |

> **Action point:** Donor agencies and host governments should consider targeted surge funding and expedited partnership agreements to lift the percentage of need met from 6.5% toward at least 15% by 2026.

## Glossary & Notes

* **Resettlement** – A durable solution whereby refugees are transferred from the country of asylum to a third country that has agreed to admit them permanently. 
* **FY 2024** – United States Federal Fiscal Year 2024 (Oct 1 2023 – Sep 30 2024). 
* **Ceiling** – The maximum number of refugees a government authorises for admission in a given year, set by legislative action (U.S.) or policy (other nations). 
* **Need vs. Capacity** – "Need" denotes the number of refugees who meet UNHCR’s eligibility criteria for resettlement; "capacity" is the actual number of admissions completed in a year.

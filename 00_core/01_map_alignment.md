# MAP Alignment - Thriving and Inclusive Communities

## What is MAP?
The Richmond Civic Hackathon is organized around the Metropolitan Action Plan (MAP) — Richmond's framework for civic priorities. This pillar, Thriving and Inclusive Communities, corresponds to Pillar 5.

## Pillar 5 alignment
Thriving and Inclusive Communities addresses the barriers that prevent all Richmond residents — including immigrant and refugee populations — from fully participating in civic and social life. Key MAP themes aligned with this pillar:

- **Equity and inclusion**: Ensuring that services reach residents who face language, cultural, and systemic barriers
- **Community resilience**: Building networks of mutual support through trusted organizations
- **Service accessibility**: Making City and partner services navigable for residents with limited English, limited digital access, or safety concerns
- **Cross-sector coordination**: Reducing duplication and fragmentation between City agencies and nonprofit partners

## Where this pillar connects to other MAP pillars
- **Affordable Housing and Homelessness** (Pillar 2): Housing instability disproportionately affects immigrant and refugee families; cross-agency navigation includes housing programs
- **Thriving Economy** (Pillar 4): Workforce programs serve immigrant and refugee residents; language access is a barrier to participation
- **Health and Well-being** (Pillar 3): Health services and behavioral health are often part of the cross-agency referral pathway; HIPAA creates data-sharing constraints
- **Safe and Just Communities** (Pillar 6): Trust between immigrant communities and public institutions is directly affected by perceived safety risks

## Hackathon scope note
This pillar's hackathon focus is narrower than the full MAP scope. Teams should focus on:
1. Help Residents Safely Discover and Connect to Trusted Support Services
2. Helping Residents Navigate the Right Support Services Without Repeating Their Story

Both problem statements are grounded in the March 6, 2026 working session findings.

## Key organizations and roles (as noted in rubric)
- **Help1RVA**: Existing service directory connecting residents to community resources
- **ReEstablish Richmond** (Kate Ayers, Helen Rai): Refugee resettlement and support
- **YMCA** (Will Thornton, Sidney Edwards): Workforce and community programming
- **Neighborhood Community Services / NCS** (Karla Almendarez-Ramos, Aneesah Smith): Community support coordination
- **FindHelp / Aunt Bertha / UniteUs**: National service directory platforms used by local organizations

Note: The rubric identifies a missing departmental champion as a quick-kill flag. Teams should be aware that post-hackathon continuation depends on a named champion from one of the above organizations or from the City.

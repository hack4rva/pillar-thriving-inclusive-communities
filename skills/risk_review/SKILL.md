# risk_review

Purpose: Prevent common civic-tech mistakes and enforce guardrails, with special attention to privacy and safety risks for vulnerable populations.

## When To Use
- Before sharing claims publicly, shipping copy, or demoing.
- When eligibility, legal, or service claims appear.
- When any data collection feature is proposed.
- Before multilingual content goes live.

## Inputs
- Product copy, UI text, README/marketing claims, demo script.
- Research outputs and cited sources.
- Privacy statement text.

## Outputs
- List of flagged risks: hallucinated services, eligibility/legal claims, missing/weak sources, misleading language, privacy risks, false safety signals.
- Concrete fixes: rewordings, source additions, scope trims.
- Privacy assessment: what data is collected and what the risk level is.
- Any open questions to resolve with officials or community organizations.

## Process
1) Check all civic claims for official sources; require links.
2) Remove/soften eligibility and legal advice; replace with "check official source".
3) Verify Richmond specificity; avoid generalizing across jurisdictions.
4) Assess privacy: does the tool collect any data from vulnerable residents? Is the privacy statement accurate?
5) Check for false safety signals: does the tool imply more protection than it provides?
6) Ensure weekend-feasible scope; cut features that require PII, new policy, or sensitive data.
7) Summarize fixes and request confirmation to apply changes.

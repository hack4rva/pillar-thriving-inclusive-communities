# Blue Sky Problem Statements - Thriving and Inclusive Communities

These are aspirational, long-horizon visions from the pillar committee working session (March 6, 2026). They are **not directly hackable** in a weekend — they require years of coordination, policy change, and sustained investment. They are included here as context for the targeted statements and to help teams understand the full ambition of the pillar.

Rubric scores are provided for each. Lower scores indicate more barriers to implementation.

---

## Blue Sky 1: A City Where Everyone Knows Where to Turn for Help

Score: 20/27 — Strong

Vision: Every Richmond resident — regardless of language, immigration status, or digital access — can quickly and safely find the right service, organization, or person to help them with any basic need.

What this requires:
- Comprehensive, multilingual, up-to-date service directory
- Outreach through channels residents actually use
- Trust-building at the community level, not just the system level
- Sustained coordination between City, nonprofits, and community organizations

Why it matters: Residents in crisis often give up before finding help because the front door is invisible or unwelcoming.

Hackathon relevance: High. The targeted problem statements are the closest weekend-buildable slice of this vision. A service discovery tool or trusted messenger system is a direct partial prototype.

---

## Blue Sky 2: A Community Support Network That Works Like a Care Team

Score: 17/27 — Needs work

Vision: Organizations serving a single resident — housing providers, case managers, workforce programs, health clinics — can coordinate seamlessly, share relevant context with consent, and avoid forcing residents to repeat their story.

What this requires:
- Shared case management infrastructure with strong consent mechanisms
- HIPAA-compliant data sharing agreements across agencies
- Willingness of organizations to participate in shared systems
- Long-term funding and staffing

Why it matters: Residents navigating multiple systems currently repeat their story to each organization, which is exhausting, often retraumatizing, and inefficient.

Hackathon relevance: Low for the full vision. The data doesn't exist and HIPAA walls are real. However, a staff-facing coordination helper (without personal resident data) is weekend-buildable.

## Blue Sky 3: Universal Access to City Services

Score: 21/27 — Strong

Vision: Every City service has a multilingual, plain-language front door that works on a phone, requires no digital literacy, and meets residents where they are — in their language and through their trusted community organizations.

What this requires:
- Multilingual content strategy across all City departments
- Voice and SMS interfaces for residents without smartphones or data plans
- Community ambassador networks to reach residents who don't trust digital tools
- Ongoing content maintenance as programs change

Why it matters: The gap between "services exist" and "residents can access them" is primarily a language, literacy, and trust problem — not a supply problem.

Hackathon relevance: High for the multilingual access component. A multilingual service finder or resource tool is a direct prototype of this vision.

---

## Blue Sky 4: A Trusted Civic Infrastructure for Community Support

Score: 13/27 — Redesign required

Vision: A city-level coordination layer that lets community organizations, nonprofits, and City agencies share service capacity, track referral outcomes, and identify gaps — all while protecting resident privacy and maintaining organizational autonomy.

What this requires:
- Major policy and data governance work
- City investment in shared infrastructure
- Trust-building between organizations that currently operate independently
- Privacy architecture that satisfies HIPAA, state, and federal requirements

Why it matters: The current fragmentation means no one can see the whole picture of unmet need.

Hackathon relevance: Very low. This is a multi-year infrastructure project. No weekend prototype credibly addresses this.

---

## Blue Sky 5: A City That Supports Immigrant and Refugee Residents as Economic Contributors

Score: Not formally scored in rubric materials

Vision: Immigrant and refugee residents in Richmond have full, visible pathways to workforce participation, small business formation, and civic engagement — supported by multilingual resources and community champions who understand both the formal systems and the cultural context.

What this requires:
- Integration of workforce, economic development, and immigration support systems
- Trusted intermediaries who bridge community and institutional knowledge
- Long-term relationship building between City, nonprofits, and community organizations

Why it matters: Immigrant and refugee residents are a significant and growing part of Richmond's economic and civic fabric. Barriers to participation hurt everyone.

Hackathon relevance: Medium. A workforce pathway tool or small business resource guide with multilingual support is buildable. The economic access angle bridges this pillar with Pillar 4 (Thriving Economy).
